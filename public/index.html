coRE: The Seven Truths of Computational Leverage

coRE

AI research and development group dedicated to exploring the frontiers of artificial intelligence. We believe in a future where human ingenuity is amplified by computational leverage.

Truth 1

AI can perform ALL cognitive tasks better than humans, when properly specified. Human role shifted to task definition rather than task execution.

Truth 2

A single human has access to unprecedented leverage.

Truth 3

Human bandwidth is the sole remaining scarce resource in an age of infinite computational leverage. Leverage is infinite, but the capacity to direct that leverage is finite, bound by the biological limits of a single human's attention, decision-making stamina, and 16 waking hours.

Truth 4

AI model access is commoditized, however the quality of the responses and the kind of questions asked is NOT a commodity. Ability to extract value from AI is still uncommon. Differentiation is in: asking better questions, recognizing quality answers, knowing how to chain interactions, having proper taste to curate outputs.

Truth 5

The new literacy is specifying, not knowing. Problem decomposition and specification as fundamental skill. Ability to precisely specify desired outcomes and recognize when they've been achieved.

Truth 6

Curation becomes a dominant form of creation. Ability to recognize excellence becomes primary creative contribution.

Truth 7

Context is the most important and impactful way to achieve aligned outputs.
